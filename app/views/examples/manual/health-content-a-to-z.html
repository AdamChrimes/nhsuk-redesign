A

Ageing

Not “aging”.

Alzheimer’s disease

We capitalise the names of conditions which start with a proper name, like Alzheimer’s disease. Note the apostrophe.

Ageing

Not “aging”.

ampersand (&)

Avoid using an ampersand, except in A&E. Use “and” instead.

antenatal

One word without a hyphen.

B

bacteria

If you give the full Latin name of a bacterium, capitalise the first word (Staphylococcus aureus) but put the shortened staphylococcus in lower case.

Do not use italics.

baseline

One word.

bottle feeding

Two words.

BSE

This stands for bovine spongiform encephalopathy but we use CJD – or variant CJD (vCJD) as the human form of BSE.

We do not use “mad cow disease”.

Use full name when you first mention CJD:  Creutzfeldt-Jakob disease.

C

caesarean

Lower case.

CJD

We use CJD – or variant CJD (vCJD) as the human form of BSE.

We do not use “mad cow disease” or BSE.

Use full name when you first mention CJD:  Creutzfeldt-Jakob disease.

clinical commissioning groups (CCGs)

Lower case, except when you’re talking about the full name of a particular CCG. For example: Anytown Clinical Commissioning Group.

CT scan

You don’t need to spell it out.

D

degrees (temperature)

We use C for centigrade or Celsius – for example, 16C or –4C.

We do not include the degrees symbol °.

See also fever (temperature).

dietitian

Not dietician.

DNA

It’s OK to use the abbreviation.

E

eardrop, eardrum, earlobe, earwax

All of them one word.

eg

eg can sometimes be read aloud as “egg” by screen readers. We use “for example” or “such as” or “like” or “including” – whichever works best.

etc

Avoid etc. Try using “for example” or “such as” or “like” or “including” instead.
