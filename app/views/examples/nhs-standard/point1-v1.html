Why it's important

Lots of products and services aren't useful for users. Others are frustrating and hard to use. By deeply understanding your users and their needs, you are more likely to help them achieve their goals.

What you must do

  • Articulate who your users are
  • Describe the problem users need you to solve
  • List the top user needs for each kind of user
  • Explain how your chosen interventions meet your user's needs
  • Have evidence that these user needs are real
  • Have evidence that your interventions work well for users

What you may want to do

  • Test for usability frequently
  • Use consistent NHS.UK design patterns
  • Follow the NHS.UK content style guide
